Friend
My idea of us does involve
those lilac nights that you mentioned -
I don't expect lilac
from saints, from lovers false
or lovers true.

There is no candor on earth but your own;
I'm telling you
that devotion is a state of suspense; suspense a state of devotion.

Whom do you love,
imagining him
in cloisters?

Island Paradise
Now and now and now at the glimmering bourn
of the only surviving coral reef,
Hugh and I
are conducting a big stakeout. We lie in wait
for another brawny red snapper, our Sunday lunch.
Something to eat.

Assurances are daily ferried on the backs of whales and dolphins to the beach,
and yet I observe that the significant others
are still bellyaching on the strand.

I detect in the surf
the ghost
of a swashbuckling teenager,
while the sun carefully updates her record of geniuses.

I have only just discovered that swimming is also reincarnation.

The fact is
too many boyfriends here do nothing but expect,
and I suspect
that the thing that they're all expecting is a new sense of self
enclosed in maudlin, brief letters
that are postmarked Los Angeles.

England Poem
Something within my aura jerks, decelerates
like either a gyroscope or a sportscar so gradually
as England swaddles her entire citizenry in breaths
of monarch butterflies and the welkin.

Speed racers in a bereft country
that dreams desserts,
not motorcycles.

Love Poem
Is it having or being
a vessel, an eye, which is a vessel,
a germ, a crystal ball,
a medium?

I have been asking. Which is it?

Limerick
It is about time to decamp
from twilight on the ocean floor,
where, nine by nine,
my pampered flaws in cute formation bob and twirl.

Sunrise,
as it has often been stated, is without ruth
or sympathy, but I guess
afternoon and evening
shall hand out rectos of peace.

Rage Poem
Everything in this sham village.
I'm silver with anger,
black with rage.
